# AI Service Delivery Operating Playbook Adapter Notes

## What This Template Is For

Use `toolkit/templates/operations-and-service-delivery/template-ai-service-delivery-operating-playbook.md` to create the city's operational delivery model for approved AI use cases.

This is the document that turns strategy and governance approval into a real service-delivery workflow.

## Minimum Readiness

Use this template when the city has:

- at least one plausible use-case domain or pilot candidate
- a provisional service owner or delivery owner
- some known governance and support dependencies

If those details are incomplete, preserve them as visible gaps rather than inventing a mature delivery organization.

## Required Local Inputs

### Required Now

- `[[CITY_NAME]]`
- one provisional service or delivery owner
- one intake path or current request channel
- one likely operational metric

### Required Before Finalization

- named ownership roles
- launch-readiness authority
- support and incident channels
- resident support model where applicable
- retirement decision authority

### Research In Parallel

- existing ticketing, help desk, or project intake systems
- incident management and service review practices
- vendor support obligations
- accessibility and language-access requirements for affected services

## Structural Rules

Keep these sections fixed:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Service Delivery Principles
- Intake And Prioritization
- Delivery Stages
- Roles And Ownership
- Testing And Readiness
- Rollout And Change Control
- Support And Incident Handling
- Vendor And Data Dependencies
- Resident Experience And Service Equity
- Metrics And Continuous Improvement
- Review And Retirement

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- treating operationalization as a small appendix to governance
- assuming every city has product managers or release engineering capacity
- skipping testing because the initial pilot is internal
- ignoring resident support because the tool is not directly public-facing
- omitting rollback and pause authority

## Adaptation Guidance

- keep pilot, limited production, and scaled production distinctions visible if the city uses them
- if roles are combined in one office, state that directly
- leave research and local-decision markers visible for service management, vendor, and resident-support gaps
- keep the playbook tied to actual delivery mechanics, not just policy statements

## Completion Criteria

The adaptation pass is complete when:

- the city can explain how an approved use case enters the delivery pipeline
- service ownership and support obligations are explicit
- testing and launch expectations are visible
- resident-impact and support obligations are visible where applicable
