# Workforce Enablement And Learning Plan Adapter Notes

## What This Template Is For

Use `toolkit/templates/workforce-and-learning/template-workforce-enablement-and-learning-plan.md` to build the city's workforce readiness layer for AI adoption.

This template should be used when the city needs to move from abstract support for AI to explicit staff capability, manager expectations, labor touchpoints, and support channels.

## Minimum Readiness

Use this template when the city can identify:

- the likely workforce groups affected first
- at least a rough view of approved or likely AI use cases
- who owns training, HR coordination, and manager enablement

Do not wait for perfect detail. The template is designed to keep unresolved decisions visible.

## Required Local Inputs

### Required Now

- `[[CITY_NAME]]`
- at least two plausible workforce segments
- one provisional training or enablement owner
- one known route for labor or HR coordination if applicable

### Required Before Finalization

- confirmed workforce segments and learning pathways
- manager monitoring expectations
- support ownership model
- workforce measures
- maintenance owner

### Research In Parallel

- bargaining-unit and employee-relations implications
- existing learning systems and training constraints
- accessibility or language-support requirements for staff training
- tool-specific guidance already in circulation

## Structural Rules

Keep these sections fixed:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Workforce Principles
- Workforce Segments
- Impact Assessment
- Learning Pathways
- Manager And Supervisor Responsibilities
- Labor, HR, And Employee-Relations Coordination
- Support Model
- Adoption Guardrails
- Measurement And Feedback
- Review And Refresh

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- treating learning as one awareness deck with no follow-through
- assuming all staff need the same depth of training
- hiding labor or HR questions until after tools are already in use
- promising role changes, efficiency savings, or staffing redesign before the city has made those decisions
- making managers accountable without giving them monitoring guidance

## Adaptation Guidance

- resolve role-title placeholders only when the city has confirmed local ownership
- keep workforce-impact depth as a visible local decision if the city is starting with pilots
- retain legal, labor, and research markers rather than forcing generic answers
- keep the plan tied to real use cases and approved workflows, not abstract AI literacy alone

## Completion Criteria

The adaptation pass is complete when:

- the city can explain who is affected first
- learning and support pathways are concrete enough to execute
- labor or employee-relations touchpoints are visible
- the workforce layer can hand off cleanly to strategy, governance, operations, and trust modules
