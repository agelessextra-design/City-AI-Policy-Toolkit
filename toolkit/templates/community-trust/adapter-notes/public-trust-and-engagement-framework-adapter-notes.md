# Public Trust And Engagement Framework Adapter Notes

## What This Template Is For

Use `toolkit/templates/community-trust/template-public-trust-and-engagement-framework.md` to build the city's reusable public-trust layer for AI.

This template should be adapted when the city needs a stable answer to:

- what residents are told
- when the city engages the public
- where questions and complaints go
- how accessibility and language access are built into trust materials

## Minimum Readiness

Use this template when the city can identify:

- at least one likely resident-facing or publicly meaningful AI use
- a provisional communications, engagement, or public-information partner
- a current or likely public inquiry or complaint pathway

If those details are still forming, leave them as visible local decisions.

## Required Local Inputs

### Required Now

- `[[CITY_NAME]]`
- one provisional public explanation channel
- one provisional inquiry or concern channel
- one accessibility or language-access point of contact

### Required Before Finalization

- confirmed notice and disclosure thresholds
- engagement threshold and ownership
- resident redress model
- trust measures
- maintenance owner

### Research In Parallel

- current public information practices
- legal disclosure obligations
- complaint, ombuds, or service recovery pathways
- accessibility and translation standards

## Structural Rules

Keep these sections fixed:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Public Trust Principles
- Public Explanation Model
- Notice And Disclosure Triggers
- Community Engagement Approach
- Accessibility And Language Access
- Questions, Concerns, And Redress
- Incident Communication
- Trust Measures
- Governance And Service Linkages
- Review And Refresh

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- treating trust as communications polish after operations are already live
- creating a public register with no useful explanation or concern pathway
- assuming all AI uses require the same level of public engagement
- omitting accessibility and language access because another city policy exists somewhere else
- hiding redress pathways behind generic customer service language

## Adaptation Guidance

- keep thresholds explicit for what gets a service-specific notice, a register entry, or a fuller public engagement process
- leave legal, accessibility, and local-decision markers visible until the city has confirmed them
- tie trust materials back to real services, not just citywide abstractions
- make sure resident feedback has a destination in governance or operations, not just a communications inbox

## Completion Criteria

The adaptation pass is complete when:

- residents would have a clear explanation path
- inquiry and concern channels are explicit
- accessibility and language-access responsibilities are visible
- trust signals flow back into governance and service improvement
