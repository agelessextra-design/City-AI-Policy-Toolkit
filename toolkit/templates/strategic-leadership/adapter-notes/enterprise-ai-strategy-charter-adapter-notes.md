# Enterprise AI Strategy Charter Adapter Notes

## What This Template Is For

Use `toolkit/templates/strategic-leadership/template-enterprise-ai-strategy-charter.md` to create the city's executive-level AI direction-setting document.

This template should answer:

- why the city is pursuing AI now
- which outcomes matter most
- who is accountable for the portfolio
- how leadership will decide what moves first
- what must be true before scale-up

It should not become a catch-all policy document. Detailed controls belong in governance, workforce, operations, and community trust artifacts.

## Minimum Readiness

Use this template when the city can identify:

- a provisional executive sponsor
- at least one plausible portfolio owner
- the strategy or performance goals AI should support
- a rough view of where pilots or early use cases may emerge

Do not force finality if those answers are not mature. Leave unresolved local-decision markers visible.

## Structural Rules

Keep these sections fixed:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Strategic Context
- Executive Mandate
- Strategic Outcomes
- Portfolio Principles
- Priority Domains
- Sequencing And Investment Logic
- Leadership Operating Model
- Dependencies On Other Toolkit Layers
- Funding And Capacity Assumptions
- Measurement And Reporting
- Review And Refresh

These sections are the minimum viable leadership operating model.

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- writing an aspiration-only strategy with no ownership or sequencing
- presenting a full enterprise rollout when the city only has pilot capacity
- treating AI as a stand-alone innovation agenda disconnected from service outcomes
- omitting workforce and community trust dependencies because they feel downstream
- assuming one elected or administrative structure fits every city

## Adaptation Guidance

- resolve identity placeholders when the city has confirmed titles
- keep `[[REQUIRES_LOCAL_DECISION: ...]]` markers visible when leadership or budget choices are still pending
- use `[[REQUIRES_LOCAL_RESEARCH: ...]]` markers when the city still needs to validate portfolio, staffing, or strategy facts
- do not smooth over political or organizational uncertainty just to make the charter read cleanly

## Completion Criteria

The adaptation pass is complete when:

- leadership ownership is explicit or visibly unresolved
- priority domains and scale-up prerequisites are understandable
- the strategy clearly links to governance, workforce, operations, and trust layers
- the document can be reviewed by leadership without pretending every downstream decision is already made
