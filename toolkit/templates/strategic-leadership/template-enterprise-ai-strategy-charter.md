# [[CITY_NAME]] Enterprise AI Strategy Charter

## Purpose

This charter defines why [[CITY_NAME]] is using artificial intelligence, what outcomes the city expects, how executive leadership will govern the effort, and how the city will sequence investment and accountability.

This charter is the strategic leadership spine for the toolkit. It should align with governance, workforce, operations, and community trust modules rather than replace them.

## Scope

This charter applies to:

- citywide AI strategy and portfolio direction
- departmental AI initiatives that require executive alignment
- cross-functional resource planning for AI work
- leadership review of public impact, workforce impact, and service impact

This charter does not replace:

- detailed governance controls
- procurement and contracting requirements
- training plans
- public-facing notices or transparency materials
- operational deployment playbooks

## Portfolio Principles

Every AI initiative under this charter should follow these principles:

- public value before novelty
- human accountability remains explicit
- governance requirements scale with risk
- workforce impact is reviewed before scale-up
- accessibility, language access, and resident trust are treated as operating requirements
- departments do not launch isolated pilots without portfolio visibility

- quarterly executive review
