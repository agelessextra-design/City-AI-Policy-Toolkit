# Review Sprint Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines how the public toolkit supports short, high-signal human review sprints after AI-assisted drafting and internal evaluation are complete.

The goal is to help a city run a bounded, role-based sprint that resolves the right issues quickly and produces a tracked revision plan.

## Scope Handled

- sprint entry criteria
- reviewer roles and responsibilities
- packet design
- pre-read workflow
- live session structure
- decision logging and revision routing
- follow-up signoff logic

## Position In The Workflow

Recommended sequence:

1. AI-assisted draft generation
2. internal evaluation and rework
3. sprint packet assembly
4. human review sprint
5. revision pass
6. targeted signoff on changed sections
7. packaging for broader circulation or adoption

## Sprint Design Principles

1. Review only what is ready.
2. Use roles, not vague committee participation.
3. Separate issue discovery from decision-making.
4. Log decisions in a reusable format.
5. Route revisions to named owners immediately.
6. Avoid reopening settled issues without new evidence.

## Sprint Entry Criteria

Do not launch a review sprint unless all of the following are true:

- the draft passed the internal review-readiness gate
- critical issues are resolved or explicitly isolated for reviewer input
- local decision points are visible and not hidden in prose
- reviewer packet is assembled
- a facilitator and scribe are named
- section ownership is clear

## Review Modes

### Mode A: Full Sprint

Use when:

- reviewing a full policy package or multiple linked modules

Typical participants:

- facilitator
- policy lead
- legal reviewer
- IT or security reviewer
- HR or labor reviewer
- operations reviewer
- communications or public trust reviewer
- scribe

### Mode B: Focused Module Sprint

Use when:

- reviewing one bounded module such as governance, training, or public transparency

Typical participants:

- facilitator
- module owner
- one to three domain reviewers
- scribe

### Mode C: Asynchronous First Pass Plus Live Reconciliation

Use when:

- reviewer calendars are constrained
- there are multiple external reviewers
- the packet is long and requires solo reading first

## Reviewer Roles

### Facilitator

Owns:

- sprint preparation
- agenda control
- conflict escalation
- pace discipline

### Policy Lead

Owns:

- intent of the document
- interpretation of toolkit structure
- revision acceptance after sprint

### Domain Reviewers

Focus:

- legal, records, procurement, privacy, employment, accessibility, trust, security, or operational fit depending on assigned role

### Scribe And Decision Logger

Owns:

- issue capture
- decision log maintenance
- unresolved-item list
- revision assignments

## Sprint Packet Contents

Minimum packet contents:

1. sprint brief
2. artifact under review
3. contradiction sweep summary
4. issue summary from evaluation
5. unresolved local decisions
6. section map with named owners
7. reviewer instructions

Optional packet contents:

- comparison to prior draft
- decision requests requiring explicit reviewer choice
- HTML pre-delivery gate summary if one was run

## Recommended Session Rules

- discuss critical issues before stylistic issues
- use the decision log in real time
- one speaker at a time, one issue at a time
- unresolved issues must have an owner and next step before closing
- avoid drafting sentence-by-sentence in the meeting unless the issue is narrow and blocking

## Decision Categories

Every live issue should end in one of these states:

- accepted change
- rejected change
- deferred for local decision
- requires legal review
- requires research
- requires rewrite and re-evaluation

## Signoff Model

Use targeted signoff rather than full re-review wherever possible.

### Full Re-Review Required When

- document structure changed materially
- new legal assumptions were introduced
- authority or escalation model changed
- major sections were added or removed

### Targeted Signoff Allowed When

- changes are localized
- issue types are clearly bounded
- the sprint routing register identifies the exact affected sections
