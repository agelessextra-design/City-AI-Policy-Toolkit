# Worked Example: Small-City Minimum Viable Five-Layer Path

## Purpose

This example shows how a small city can use the full toolkit without pretending it has the staffing depth, specialized governance offices, or operational redundancy of a larger municipality.

It is a worked adaptation path, not final city policy text.

## Example Scenario

City profile for this example:

- small city with a lean administrative team
- limited dedicated innovation capacity
- IT, HR, communications, and legal support shared across multiple portfolios
- a few AI use cases emerging through vendor products and informal staff experimentation
- strong need for a simple but explicit control structure

## Starting Problem

The city needs a minimum viable municipal AI system that is small enough to maintain but still clear about approval, staff expectations, operational ownership, and resident-facing accountability.

## Recommended Module Sequence

### 1. Governance And Policy First

Why:

- the city needs one simple approval and accountability model before activity spreads
- lean staffing means ambiguity will quickly become unowned risk

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/governance-and-policy/template-governance-core-policy.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/governance-and-policy/prompt-pack-governance-pilot.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-module-family-scorecard.md`

### 2. Strategic Leadership Second

Why:

- even a small city needs an executive statement of scope, limits, and sequencing
- a short leadership artifact helps prevent fragmented department-by-department exceptions

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/strategic-leadership/template-enterprise-ai-strategy-charter.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/strategic-leadership/prompt-pack-strategic-leadership.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/guide-module-family-review-readiness.md`

### 3. Workforce And Learning Third

Why:

- a small city often depends on managers wearing multiple hats
- staff guidance must be short, practical, and easy to supervise

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/workforce-and-learning/template-workforce-enablement-and-learning-plan.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/workforce-and-learning/prompt-pack-workforce-learning.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-review-readiness-summary.md`

### 4. Operations And Service Delivery Fourth

Why:

- operational safeguards can stay lightweight, but they cannot stay implicit
- the city needs one shared launch, pause, and support path for a small number of pilots

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/operations-and-service-delivery/template-ai-service-delivery-operating-playbook.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/operations-and-service-delivery/prompt-pack-operations-service-delivery.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-review-sprint-brief.md`

### 5. Community Trust Fifth

Why:

- smaller cities often rely on informal trust, but resident-facing AI still needs explicit notice and support
- the trust layer should stay simple enough for a lean communications function to maintain

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/community-trust/template-public-trust-and-engagement-framework.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/community-trust/prompt-pack-community-trust.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-board-review-packet.md`

## Minimum Viable Build Rules

- keep the first pass short enough for one cross-functional city team to maintain
- centralize approval logic before multiplying templates
- preserve placeholders rather than inventing local authorities the city does not have
- use the evaluation and review assets to expose thin staffing assumptions early
