# City-Scale Adaptation Starter Comparison

## Purpose

This matrix compares how a small city, midsize city, and large city might start with the toolkit.

It is not a claim that every city in a scale bucket should behave the same way. It is a starter comparison to help users pick a sane starting path.

## Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Small City | Midsize City | Large City |
|---|---|---|---|
| Likely first bottleneck | missing shared governance and capacity | scattered pilots without coherent system logic | portfolio complexity and cross-department coordination |
| Best starting family | governance and policy | strategic leadership | strategic leadership plus governance in close sequence |
| Workforce pattern | limited formal training capacity, combined roles | emerging training and manager oversight needs | multiple workforce groups, stronger labor and supervisor complexity |
| Operations pattern | lighter pilot load, fewer formal release layers | pilots need explicit launch and support discipline | more formal operational ownership, service tiers, and vendor dependencies |
| Community-trust pattern | likely lightweight but still explicit trust path | service notices and resident support become material quickly | broader public accountability, accessibility, and communications structure |
| Main risk if started badly | generic policy with no operating owner | growth of pilots without shared controls | fragmented enterprise system with contradictory modules |

## Practical Use

- use this matrix to choose where to start
- do not treat it as a replacement for local context discovery
- keep local differences visible even when the city resembles one of these patterns
