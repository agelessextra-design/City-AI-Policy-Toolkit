# Worked Example: Large-City Enterprise Five-Layer Path

## Purpose

This example shows how a large city can use the toolkit as an enterprise operating system instead of treating the five module families as isolated documents.

It is a worked system path, not final city policy text.

## Example Scenario

City profile for this example:

- large city with multiple departments running or proposing AI uses
- mayoral, administrative, legal, procurement, and digital-service functions all involved
- stronger public scrutiny, media exposure, and accessibility expectations
- a need to sequence many pilots without producing contradictory department-level rules

## Starting Problem

The city needs a citywide AI operating model that can coordinate executive direction, risk governance, workforce controls, delivery discipline, and resident accountability across a complex portfolio.

## Recommended Module Sequence

### 1. Strategic Leadership First

Why:

- a large city needs portfolio logic before departments optimize locally
- the leadership layer must define scaling conditions, sequencing, and enterprise sponsorship

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/strategic-leadership/template-enterprise-ai-strategy-charter.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/strategic-leadership/prompt-pack-strategic-leadership.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-module-family-scorecard.md`

### 2. Governance And Policy Second

Why:

- portfolio direction must convert into review tiers, approval rights, and escalation paths
- governance has to work across multiple departments, vendors, and public risk profiles

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/governance-and-policy/template-governance-core-policy.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/governance-and-policy/prompt-pack-governance-pilot.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/guide-module-family-review-readiness.md`

### 3. Workforce And Learning Third

Why:

- a large city needs coordinated expectations across executives, managers, front-line staff, and specialized operators
- labor, supervision, and training content must align with the leadership and governance layers

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/workforce-and-learning/template-workforce-enablement-and-learning-plan.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/workforce-and-learning/prompt-pack-workforce-learning.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-review-readiness-summary.md`

### 4. Operations And Service Delivery Fourth

Why:

- large-city pilots often involve more dependencies, vendors, release controls, and monitoring obligations
- the operations layer must translate approvals into repeatable launch and support mechanics

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/operations-and-service-delivery/template-ai-service-delivery-operating-playbook.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/operations-and-service-delivery/prompt-pack-operations-service-delivery.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-review-sprint-routing-register.md`

### 5. Community Trust Fifth

Why:

- resident-facing accountability is wider and more formal in a large city
- the trust layer must connect notice, support, accessibility, and escalation back to governance and operations

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/community-trust/template-public-trust-and-engagement-framework.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/community-trust/prompt-pack-community-trust.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-unresolved-major-issue-escalation-brief.md`

## Enterprise Build Rules

- sequence strategy and governance tightly so enterprise direction becomes operational control
- make interdepartmental ownership and escalation visible before pilots scale
- treat workforce, operations, and trust as production layers, not communication add-ons
- use cross-system evaluation before any board-style or executive review packet is assembled
