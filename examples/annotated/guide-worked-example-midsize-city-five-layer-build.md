# Worked Example: Midsize City Five-Layer Build

## Purpose

This example shows how a midsize city could use the toolkit across all five module families instead of treating the toolkit as only a governance engine.

It is a worked build map, not final city policy text.

## Example Scenario

City profile for this example:

- midsize city with several departments already experimenting with AI
- no unified enterprise AI charter yet
- governance controls are emerging but incomplete
- staff training is uneven
- operations teams want to launch pilots quickly
- public-trust posture is still being defined

## Starting Problem

The city wants to move from scattered AI experimentation to a coherent municipal AI system without freezing innovation or hiding risk.

## Recommended Module Sequence

### 1. Strategic Leadership First

Why:

- the city needs executive direction before pilots multiply
- portfolio sequencing and scale-up conditions are unclear

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/strategic-leadership/template-enterprise-ai-strategy-charter.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/strategic-leadership/prompt-pack-strategic-leadership.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-module-family-scorecard.md`

### 2. Governance And Policy Second

Why:

- leadership direction needs approval pathways, accountability, and risk controls
- departments need one citywide control model

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/governance-and-policy/template-governance-core-policy.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/governance-and-policy/prompt-pack-governance-pilot.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/guide-module-family-review-readiness.md`

### 3. Workforce And Learning Third

Why:

- staff adoption is already happening
- managers and HR need a supervised enablement path

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/workforce-and-learning/template-workforce-enablement-and-learning-plan.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/workforce-and-learning/prompt-pack-workforce-learning.md`
- `toolkit/evaluations/template-review-readiness-summary.md`

### 4. Operations And Service Delivery Fourth

Why:

- some pilots are ready to move from idea to operational launch
- service ownership, testing, support, and rollback need to be explicit

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/operations-and-service-delivery/template-ai-service-delivery-operating-playbook.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/operations-and-service-delivery/prompt-pack-operations-service-delivery.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-review-sprint-routing-register.md`

### 5. Community Trust Fifth

Why:

- resident-facing pilots require notice, support, and accountability
- the city needs a trust layer before broad external rollout

Representative artifacts:

- `toolkit/templates/community-trust/template-public-trust-and-engagement-framework.md`
- `toolkit/prompts/community-trust/prompt-pack-community-trust.md`
- `toolkit/review-sprint-kit/template-board-review-packet.md`

## Shared Workflow Path

Each module family still follows the same phase logic:

1. intake
2. outline
3. draft
4. evaluation
5. review sprint
6. revision and targeted signoff

Hardening passes then use the family-specific hardening prompt packs.

## Cross-Family Dependency Map

- strategy names what may scale and under what conditions
- governance defines what must be approved and monitored
- workforce defines how staff and managers are prepared
- operations defines how pilots launch, pause, and improve
- community trust defines what residents are told and where concerns go

## What This Example Proves

- the toolkit can now support a five-layer build path, not only governance-family work
- the module families can be sequenced by dependency rather than by folder order
- a midsize-city user can see how templates, prompts, evaluation, and review fit together

## What This Example Does Not Prove Yet

- detailed small-city simplification choices
- large-city enterprise operating-model complexity
- final city-facing policy quality
- full public example packet quality
